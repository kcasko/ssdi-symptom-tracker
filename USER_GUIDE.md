## Core Features

### 1. Daily Symptom Logging

**Purpose**: Track symptoms you experience each day with severity ratings.

#### How to Log Symptoms:

1. Tap **"Daily Log"** from the Dashboard
2. **Select Date**: Defaults to today (can change if logging retroactively)
3. **Select Symptoms**: Choose from 60+ symptoms across 6 categories:
   - Pain symptoms (headache, joint pain, muscle pain, etc.)
   - Neurological symptoms (brain fog, dizziness, numbness, etc.)
   - Gastrointestinal symptoms (nausea, diarrhea, constipation, etc.)
   - Respiratory symptoms (shortness of breath, cough, etc.)
   - Psychological symptoms (anxiety, depression, panic, etc.)
   - Energy/Fatigue symptoms (chronic fatigue, weakness, etc.)

4. **Rate Severity**: For each symptom, use the 0-10 scale:
   - **0**: No symptom
   - **1-3**: Mild (noticeable but manageable)
   - **4-6**: Moderate (interferes with activities)
   - **7-9**: Severe (significantly limits function)
   - **10**: Extreme (completely disabling)

5. **Add Context** (optional but recommended):
   - What triggered the symptom?
   - How long did it last?
   - What helped or made it worse?

6. **General Notes**: Overall day summary, patterns noticed

7. **Add Photos** (optional): Visual evidence of swelling, rashes, mobility aids, etc.

8. **Save** your log

**Pro Tips**:
- ‚úÖ Log symptoms **the same day** they occur for accuracy
- ‚úÖ Be **specific** in notes ("couldn't lift left arm above shoulder")
- ‚úÖ Use **functional language** ("unable to" rather than "painful")
- ‚úÖ Include **duration** (e.g., "fatigue lasted 6 hours after activity")

---

### 2. Activity Impact Logging

**Purpose**: Document how activities affect your symptoms and limitations.

#### How to Log Activities:

1. Tap **"Activity Log"** from the Dashboard
2. **Select Activity**: Choose from 50+ activities across 5 categories:
   - Physical Activities (walking, lifting, standing, etc.)
   - Household Tasks (cooking, cleaning, laundry, etc.)
   - Self-Care (bathing, dressing, grooming, etc.)
   - Social Activities (conversations, gatherings, etc.)
   - Cognitive Tasks (reading, focusing, decision-making, etc.)

3. **Duration**: How long you did the activity (minutes)

4. **Impact Severity**: Rate 0-10 how much the activity worsened symptoms

5. **Stopped Early?**: Indicate if you couldn't complete the activity

6. **Activity Context**: Notes about:
   - What happened during the activity
   - How long recovery took
   - Assistance needed
   - Modifications required

7. **Save** your log

**Pro Tips**:
- ‚úÖ Log activities **immediately after** or the same day
- ‚úÖ Note if you needed **rest breaks** (e.g., "rested 3 times during 10-minute walk")
- ‚úÖ Document **assistance needed** (e.g., "spouse had to help with buttons")
- ‚úÖ Track **recovery time** (e.g., "exhausted for 2 days after grocery shopping")

---

### 3. Functional Limitations

**Purpose**: Document long-term limitations in Activities of Daily Living (ADLs).

#### How to Document Limitations:

1. Navigate to **"Limitations"** screen
2. Tap **"Add Limitation"**
3. **Select Category**:
   - Mobility (walking, standing, sitting, bending)
   - Lifting/Carrying (weight limits, assistance needed)
   - Fine Motor Skills (writing, buttoning, gripping)
   - Cognitive Function (memory, concentration, following instructions)
   - Social Function (interactions, crowds, stress tolerance)
   - Self-Care (bathing, dressing, eating, hygiene)

4. **Describe the Limitation**: Use functional language
   - ‚ùå Bad: "Back hurts"
   - ‚úÖ Good: "Can stand for max 10 minutes before needing to sit/lie down"

5. **Since When**: When the limitation started

6. **Supporting Evidence**: Link to related symptom logs or photos

7. **Save** the limitation

**Pro Tips**:
- ‚úÖ Be **quantitative** (e.g., "can lift max 5 lbs" not "can't lift much")
- ‚úÖ Compare to **before illness** (e.g., "used to walk 3 miles, now max 50 feet")
- ‚úÖ Note **frequency** (e.g., "happens 4-5 days per week")
- ‚úÖ Document **consistency** (e.g., "unable to work full 8-hour day reliably")

## Evidence Mode

**Evidence Mode** is an advanced feature that adds **legal documentation standards** to your logs for SSDI submission.

### What is Evidence Mode?

Evidence Mode adds:
- üïê **Evidence Timestamps**: Immutable timestamps when logs are created
- üîí **Log Finalization**: Lock logs to prevent accidental changes
- üìú **Revision Tracking**: Audit trail of any changes to finalized logs
- üì¶ **Submission Packs**: Structured PDF packages for legal submission

### When to Enable Evidence Mode

**Enable Evidence Mode when**:
- You're preparing for SSDI application
- You anticipate appeals or hearings
- You want to maintain evidence integrity
- Your attorney recommends formal documentation

**Don't enable if**:
- You're just casually tracking symptoms
- You're still figuring out your condition
- You make frequent corrections to logs

### How to Enable Evidence Mode

1. Navigate to **Settings**
2. Find **"Evidence Documentation"** section
3. Toggle **"Enable Evidence Mode"**
4. Read and confirm the implications:
   - All new logs will receive evidence timestamps
   - You can finalize logs to make them read-only
   - Changes to finalized logs create revision records

### Evidence Timestamps

**What are they?**
- Timestamps automatically applied to new logs when Evidence Mode is ON
- Shows when the evidence was documented (not when it occurred)
- Cannot be removed once applied

**Why they matter**:
- Demonstrates **contemporaneous documentation** (recorded near the time it happened)
- Shows **consistency** in tracking
- Provides **chronological evidence** trail

**How to see them**:
- Look for "Evidence recorded: [timestamp]" on log screens

### Finalizing Logs

**What is finalization?**
- Marks a log as "complete and accurate"
- Prevents accidental edits or deletions
- Creates a locked record for evidence purposes

**How to finalize**:
1. Open a daily or activity log
2. Review it for accuracy
3. Tap **"Finalize for Evidence"**
4. Confirm you've reviewed the log

**When to finalize**:
- After you've reviewed the log for accuracy
- Before including it in a submission pack
- When your attorney requests locked evidence

**What happens when finalized**:
- ‚úÖ Log becomes read-only
- ‚úÖ Shows "Finalized" badge
- ‚úÖ Records who finalized and when
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Cannot be directly edited (must use revision system)

### Revision Tracking

**What if I need to correct a finalized log?**

Evidence Mode uses a **revision system**:

1. Attempt to edit a finalized log
2. System blocks direct editing
3. Use **"Create Revision"** option instead
4. Provide:
   - Changes to be made
   - Reason for revision
   - Your reviewer notes

5. Revision is recorded with:
   - Timestamp of revision
   - Who made it
   - What changed
   - Why it changed

**Viewing Revision History**:
1. Open a finalized log
2. Tap **"View [X] Revisions"** link
3. Modal shows chronological list of all changes

**Why revisions matter**:
- Maintains **audit trail** for legal proceedings
- Shows **transparency** (not hiding changes)
- Demonstrates **accuracy commitment**

### Submission Packs

**What is a Submission Pack?**

A submission pack is a **structured PDF package** containing:
- Cover page with claimant information
- Table of contents
- Daily logs in standardized narrative format
- Activity logs
- Functional limitations
- Medication list
- Appointment history
- Photo evidence
- RFC (Residual Functional Capacity) summary
- SSA Form 3368 data

**How to Create a Submission Pack**:

1. Navigate to **Settings** ‚Üí "Evidence Documentation"
   OR **Reports** ‚Üí "Evidence Submission Packs"

2. Configure the pack:
   - **Date Range**: Select the period to include (e.g., last 90 days)
   - **Symptom Filters**: Choose specific symptoms to include (or all)
   - **Include Only Finalized Logs**: Option to include only locked evidence
   - **Include Photos**: Attach photo evidence
   - **Include Medical Records**: Attach appointment summaries

3. Tap **"Generate Submission Pack"**

4. Review the pack metadata:
   - Number of logs included
   - Date coverage
   - Symptoms documented

5. Tap **"Export PDF"**

6. Save the PDF to your device or share directly

**When to create a submission pack**:
- Initial SSDI application submission
- Reconsideration or appeal
- Attorney request for evidence compilation
- Hearing preparation

**Pro Tips**:
- ‚úÖ Create packs for **specific time periods** (e.g., 90 days, 6 months)
- ‚úÖ Use **multiple packs** for different symptom categories if needed
- ‚úÖ Always **review the PDF** before submitting
- ‚úÖ Have your **attorney review** submission packs before filing
- ‚úÖ Keep **backup copies** of all packs

## Generating Reports

### Report Types

The app generates 4 types of reports:

1. **Daily Summary Report**: Focuses on day-to-day symptom patterns
2. **Activity Impact Report**: Emphasizes functional limitations from activities
3. **RFC (Residual Functional Capacity) Report**: Structured for SSA's RFC assessment
4. **Complete Narrative Report**: Comprehensive documentation of all aspects

### How to Generate a Report

1. Navigate to **Reports** screen
2. Select **Date Range**:
   - Last 30 days
   - Last 90 days
   - Last 6 months
   - Custom range

3. Choose **Template**:
   - Full Narrative (recommended for SSDI)
   - Daily Log Summary
   - Activity Impact Analysis
   - RFC Builder

4. Tap **"Generate Report"**

5. Wait for processing (may take 10-30 seconds for large datasets)

6. Report appears in **"Saved Reports"** list

### Viewing and Editing Reports

1. Tap a saved report to open **Report Editor**

2. Review each section:
   - **Profile Summary**: Demographics and overview
   - **Symptom Analysis**: Patterns, frequency, severity
   - **Activity Impact**: Functional limitations
   - **Medication Compliance**: Treatment history
   - **RFC Assessment**: Work capacity evaluation

3. **Edit sections** if needed:
   - Tap on a section to edit
   - Use SSDI-friendly language
   - Add context or clarifications
   - Preserve source data (don't delete references)

4. **Save** changes

### Exporting Reports

1. Open a report in Report Editor
2. Tap **"Export"** button
3. Choose format:
   - **PDF** (recommended for submission)
   - **Text** (for copying into forms)
   - **JSON** (for data backup)

4. Share via:
   - Email to attorney
   - Save to cloud storage
   - Print directly
   - Share to SSA online portal (if applicable)

**Pro Tips**:
- ‚úÖ Generate reports **every 30-90 days** to track progress
- ‚úÖ Review reports with your **attorney** before submission
- ‚úÖ Keep **multiple versions** (e.g., 3-month, 6-month, 12-month)
- ‚úÖ Include reports with **reconsideration** and **appeal** requests

1. Navigate to **Reports** screen

3. Tap **"Full Backup (JSON)"**

   - Cloud storage (Google Drive, iCloud, Dropbox)

## Tips for SSDI Success

### Documentation Best Practices

**1. Consistency is Key**
- ‚úÖ Log symptoms **every day** or at least 5 days/week
- ‚úÖ Create a **routine** (e.g., log before bed)
- ‚úÖ Don't skip "good days" (SSA needs to see variability)

**2. Be Specific and Quantitative**
- ‚ùå "I was tired" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Fatigue prevented me from preparing meals; needed to lie down for 3 hours"
- ‚ùå "Pain was bad" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Back pain 8/10; couldn't sit for more than 10 minutes"
- ‚ùå "Hard to concentrate" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Brain fog prevented me from reading more than 1 paragraph without forgetting content"

**3. Use Functional Language**
- Focus on **what you cannot do**, not just symptoms
- Example: Instead of "knee pain," write "cannot stand for more than 5 minutes due to knee instability; required sitting breaks every 5 minutes during grocery shopping"

**4. Document Everything**
- ‚úÖ Medications and side effects
- ‚úÖ Doctor visits and recommendations
- ‚úÖ Assistive devices used (cane, walker, shower chair, etc.)
- ‚úÖ Help needed from family/caregivers
- ‚úÖ Activities you've stopped doing

**5. Track Patterns**
- Note **triggers** (weather, activity, stress)
- Document **frequency** (daily, 3x/week, monthly flares)
- Record **duration** (symptoms last 2 hours, 3 days, constant)
- Show **impact** (missed work, cancelled plans, needed ER visit)

### Working with Attorneys

**How the app helps attorneys**:
- Provides **chronological evidence** trail
- Generates **professional narratives** in SSDI language
- Exports **structured data** for SSA forms
- Maintains **audit trail** with Evidence Mode

**What to share with your attorney**:
1. **Full Narrative Reports** (6-12 months)
2. **Submission Packs** (if using Evidence Mode)
3. **CSV exports** (for their own analysis)
4. **RFC reports** (for form completion)

**When to involve your attorney**:
- Before finalizing logs for evidence
- Before creating submission packs
- When generating reports for hearings
- If you need specific date ranges or formats

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå **Logging only bad days** ‚Üí Log good days too; SSA needs to see your baseline  
‚ùå **Vague descriptions** ‚Üí Be specific with numbers, durations, limitations  
‚ùå **Skipping context** ‚Üí Always explain how symptoms impact function  
‚ùå **Not backing up data** ‚Üí Back up weekly; losing months of logs is devastating  
‚ùå **Waiting to enable Evidence Mode** ‚Üí Enable it early if planning SSDI application  
‚ùå **Not reviewing reports before submission** ‚Üí Always review; generated text may need clarification  
‚ùå **Ignoring medication compliance** ‚Üí SSA expects you to follow prescribed treatment  
‚ùå **Logging retrospectively weeks later** ‚Üí Log same-day for accuracy and credibility

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

#### "App won't start" or crashes on launch
**Solutions**:
- Restart your device
- Update to the latest app version
- Clear app cache (Settings ‚Üí Apps ‚Üí SSDI Symptom Tracker ‚Üí Clear Cache)
- If persists, report issue on GitHub

#### "Data disappeared" after update
**Solutions**:
- Check if you're viewing the correct profile (Profile Picker)
- Data may be intact but profile switched
- Restore from backup if you have one
- Data migration issues are rare but possible; report on GitHub

#### "Can't edit a log"
**Solutions**:
- Check if log is **finalized** in Evidence Mode
- Use **revision system** instead of direct editing
- If not finalized and still can't edit, report issue

#### "Reports not generating" or stuck
**Solutions**:
- Ensure you have logs in the selected date range
- Try a smaller date range (30 days instead of 6 months)
- Close and restart the app
- Check device storage space

#### "Export/PDF not working"
**Solutions**:
- Grant storage permissions if prompted
- Ensure enough device storage space
- Try exporting smaller datasets
- Update the app to latest version

#### "Evidence Mode toggle not working"
**Solutions**:
- Ensure you have an active profile selected
- Restart the app
- Check that you've confirmed the Evidence Mode dialog

## Appendix

### SSDI Glossary

**ADLs (Activities of Daily Living)**: Basic self-care tasks (bathing, dressing, eating, etc.)  
**RFC (Residual Functional Capacity)**: What work activities you can still perform despite limitations  
**Substantial Gainful Activity (SGA)**: Earnings threshold for SSDI eligibility  
**Onset Date**: When your disability began  
**Listings**: SSA's list of medical conditions that automatically qualify for disability  
**Consultative Exam (CE)**: SSA-ordered medical exam if your records are insufficient  

### Symptom Categories Reference

- **Pain**: 12 types (headache, back pain, joint pain, nerve pain, etc.)
- **Neurological**: 10 types (brain fog, dizziness, tremor, numbness, etc.)
- **Gastrointestinal**: 8 types (nausea, diarrhea, constipation, etc.)
- **Respiratory**: 6 types (shortness of breath, cough, wheezing, etc.)
- **Psychological**: 14 types (anxiety, depression, panic, PTSD symptoms, etc.)
- **Energy/Fatigue**: 8 types (chronic fatigue, post-exertional malaise, weakness, etc.)

### Activity Categories Reference

- **Physical**: 15 activities (walking, lifting, carrying, climbing, etc.)
- **Household**: 12 tasks (cooking, cleaning, laundry, shopping, etc.)
- **Self-Care**: 8 tasks (bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, etc.)
- **Social**: 7 activities (conversations, crowds, social events, etc.)
- **Cognitive**: 10 tasks (reading, writing, math, following instructions, etc.)

### SSA Forms This App Helps With

- **SSA-3368**: Disability Report - Adult
- **SSA-827**: Authorization to Release Information
- **SSA-3380**: Function Report (Adult)
- **RFC Forms**: Physical and Mental Residual Functional Capacity assessments
